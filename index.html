Security Awareness

Spotting Fake Senders

Always check the sender's email address carefully. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to official ones (e.g., support@paypaI.com instead of paypal.com) or use generic public domains like @gmail.com for official business.

Hover Before You Click

Hover your mouse cursor over links in the email to see the actual destination URL. If the URL looks strange, contains random characters, or uses an IP address instead of a domain name, do not click it.

Beware of Urgency

Phishing emails often try to create a sense of panic or urgency to make you act quickly without thinking. Phrases like "immediate action required" or "your account will be suspended" are common red flags.

Never Share Credentials

Legitimate organizations will never ask for your password, PIN, or full Social Security number via email. Treat any email requesting this information as highly suspicious.
